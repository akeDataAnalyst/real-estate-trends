# UAE Real Estate Market Trend Analysis for First-Time Buyers

## Project Overview

This end-to-end project simulates a market intelligence report for a real estate company expanding into the UAE (Dubai & Abu Dhabi focus).  

It analyzes residential transaction trends (2023–2025), with special emphasis on first-time home buyers (mid-income end-users), Pricing structures, Sustainability features, and buyer preferences — directly aligned with key responsibilities in real estate marketing research roles.

### Key Outcomes & Business Value

- Quantified **9% price premium** for properties with sustainability/livability features (robust OLS regression result, p < 0.001)
- Confirmed **31–33%** higher price per sqft in Dubai vs Abu Dhabi
- Identified **mid-range segment (AED 0.8–4.5M)** as a first-time buyer stronghold with reduced investor price competition
- Delivered **actionable recommendations**: target mid-income first-time buyers with sustainable mid-tier developments → potential **10–15% ROI uplift**

## Key Findings Summary

- Sustainability Premium: 9% higher price per sqft (coef 0.087–0.110, p < 0.001)
- Dubai Premium: 31–33% higher than Abu Dhabi
- Buyer Dynamics: First-Time buyers (59% of transactions) dominate mid-range (AED 0.8–4.5M); Investors pay less per sqft in this segment (negative interaction)
- Time Trend: 20% cumulative increase by 2025 vs 2023

## Strategic Recommendations

- Target: Mid-income first-time buyers (AED 0.8–4.5M apartments/townhouses)
- Positioning: Emphasize sustainability, livability, community features
- Geography: Dubai mid-tier areas (JVC, Business Bay, Dubai Hills); secondary Abu Dhabi
- Pricing: 5–10% premium for green features; align with government first-time buyer programs
- Impact: Higher conversions + pricing power → 10–15% potential ROI improvement
